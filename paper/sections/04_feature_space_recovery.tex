\section{Feature-Space Exploration}
\label{sec:feature}

This section addresses \textbf{RQ2} by asking what frozen features themselves reveal. The repository contains a probe based on clustering pooled coarsest \texttt{backbone\_fpn} features, with coarse-only and flip-averaged variants plus documented scale and positionality controls. That probe matters scientifically because it is the lightest possible readout: no decoder is trained, and the backbone remains untouched.

We interpret this route under its own archived probe protocol rather than as a direct leaderboard comparison to the matched proposal-route tables. Appendix~\ref{app:feature_provenance} reports the repository-backed parity summary, a curated qualitative gallery, and scale-weighting diagnostics. Read together, those artifacts support a concrete claim: coarse frozen features appear to contain nontrivial texture structure, and the probe is sensitive to positional debiasing such as flip-averaging.

This route is informative precisely because the readout is so simple. When clustering pooled coarse features already separates repeated-pattern regions, transition cases, and shape-versus-texture examples, it becomes hard to argue that frozen SAM is entirely texture-blind. What the feature route does not settle by itself is how much of that latent structure has already been externalized into the generated masks under matched benchmark evaluation. That complementary question is the subject of the next section.
