\section{Benchmarks and Evaluation Protocol}
\label{sec:data}

The paper's central benchmark story now has four proposal-space routes plus one appendix real-world complement. Two routes are historical anchors: the Real-World Texture Dataset (RWTD) on the natural side and the STLD benchmark on the synthetic side. Those two remain gold-standard comparison points, but by themselves they do not span broader public natural-image texture crops or more naturalistic synthetic transitions. We therefore also evaluate the natural side on our separately released curated ADE20K textural-crop subset~\cite{detextureade20k2026} and the synthetic side on our ControlNet-based generative bridge benchmark, which preserves exact binary supervision while introducing more naturalistic transitions. CAID moves to the appendix as a complementary real-world shoreline benchmark whose exact masks and unusually large contiguous land/water partitions probe a distinct texture-like structure in remote-sensing / overhead imagery~\cite{wang2025caid}.

\paragraph{RWTD.} RWTD is the paper's anchor benchmark because it concentrates the failure modes that motivate recoverability: weak transitions, disconnected same-texture regions, and repeated-pattern distractors. Main results use the official evaluator plus a matched shared subset for direct comparison to the public TextureSAM rerun.

\paragraph{ADE20K-selected natural complement.} The ADE20K complement uses our separately released curated refined-crop subset~\cite{detextureade20k2026}, which contains 753 paired textural crop tests, corresponding to about 1.5K labeled texture regions across the two sides. In this paper it serves only as a broader natural-image complement to the historical anchor pair. For strict comparison against the released TextureSAM checkpoint, the paper reports the validation slice of that curated subset. Because the two texture sides are label-symmetric, its primary readout is partition-invariant mIoU / ARI. Appendix~\ref{app:detexture_secondary} records the route provenance, secondary metric views, and qualitative cases.

\paragraph{STLD.} STLD is the historical synthetic companion. It is useful here because the shaped foreground side is canonical and exact supervision is available. That makes it a clean test of what the generated mask bank already reveals when evaluator ambiguity is minimal.

\paragraph{ControlNet bridge synthetic complement.} The ControlNet bridge route preserves exact binary labels while replacing classical hard mosaics with more naturalistic stitched transitions. It is therefore a synthetic complement to STLD rather than a replacement for it: STLD retains the canonical shaped foreground, while the bridge route tests whether the same recoverability story survives under more modern synthetic image statistics. Appendix~\ref{app:bridge_secondary} records the generation provenance, secondary metric views, and qualitative cases.

\paragraph{Appendix real-world complement.} CAID is kept in the appendix because it is structurally distinctive rather than central: shoreline partitions are real-world, exact, and texture-like, but they emphasize large contiguous land/water regions rather than the smaller-scale natural texture transitions that define the main paper's benchmark center.

\paragraph{Metrics and subset conventions.} RWTD uses official mIoU and ARI under complementary binary partitions. STLD uses direct foreground mIoU and ARI because the shaped foreground side is canonical. The ADE20K-selected complement, the ControlNet bridge complement, and CAID use partition-invariant mIoU and ARI because their two sides are label-symmetric for the binary partition task. Every headline table keeps evaluator and subset conventions fixed within each comparison block; unmatched legacy numbers are not mixed into the main results.
