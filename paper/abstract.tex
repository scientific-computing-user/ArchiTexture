\begin{abstract}
Does frozen SAM contain texture understanding even though it was built mainly for semantic segmentation? Recent fine-tuning results suggest that texture-relevant structure may already exist inside the frozen model. We investigate that possibility through two complementary routes. Feature-space exploration asks what is already latent in frozen multiscale features; proposal-/mask-space exploration asks what is already recoverable from the generated mask bank without retraining the backbone or proposal generator. We study both routes on the field's standard state-of-the-art real-world and synthesized texture benchmarks, and then extend that benchmark picture with a new ADE20K textural curation and a stitched synthetic bridge benchmark. The feature route reveals that coarse frozen features already support plausible texture partitions and that positional debiasing such as flip-averaging materially affects what those features reveal. The proposal route shows that generated masks also contain substantial recoverable texture structure: it outperforms a public TextureSAM rerun on both the standard real-world and synthesized benchmarks, and the same reading carries to the broader natural-image and stitched-synthetic complements. Together, the two routes indicate that frozen SAM contains texture-relevant structure in both representation and generated-mask space, while the broadened benchmark set shows that this recoverability story is not confined to one historical natural/synthetic pair alone. \codelinksentence
\end{abstract}
