\begin{abstract}
Does frozen SAM contain texture understanding even though it was built mainly for semantic segmentation? Recent fine-tuning results suggest that texture-relevant structure may already exist inside the frozen system. We investigate that possibility through two complementary routes. Feature-space exploration asks what is already latent in frozen multiscale features; proposal-/mask-space exploration asks what is already recoverable from the generated mask bank without retraining the backbone or proposal generator. The feature route reveals that coarse frozen features already support plausible texture partitions and that positional debiasing such as flip-averaging materially affects what those features reveal. The proposal route shows that generated masks also contain substantial recoverable texture structure: a lightweight learned readout above the frozen mask bank improves RWTD common-253 coherence from 0.6163 to 0.7013 ARI against a public TextureSAM rerun while keeping mIoU comparable, and improves STLD common-182 from 0.5140/0.7526 to 0.7195/0.7791. Oracle analysis further shows unrecovered headroom in the frozen bank, reaching 0.5183 mIoU / 0.8580 ARI on RWTD. Together, the two routes indicate that frozen SAM contains texture-relevant structure in both representation and generated-mask space, although ambiguity handling above fragmented proposals remains unresolved. \codelinksentence
\end{abstract}
